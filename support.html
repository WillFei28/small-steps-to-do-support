Description

Overcome Procrastination, One Step at a Time.

Feeling overwhelmed by a huge to-do list? Small Steps To-Do is the simple, powerful productivity app designed specifically for students, individuals with Procrastination, ADHD, or anyone struggling with task initiation and follow-through.

We turn daunting goals into clear, bite-sized tasks, reducing stress and boosting your sense of accomplishment.

How Small Steps To-Do Works (Powered by AI)

  1. Input Your Goal: Simply tell the app what you need to study or complete.
  2. AI Breaks It Down: Our backend system, powered by AI, instantly analyzes your input and transforms it into a structured, step-by-step plan with suggested time durations.
  3. Visual Progress Tracking: Focus only on the current, small step. As you check off each step, you fill a star in your main task tile, giving you an immediate, encouraging visual reward and eliminating the pressure of the big task.
  4. Stay Focused & Clean: The interface is designed to be clean and simple, minimizing animations and distractions so you can focus on doing.

Key Features:

  • AI-Powered Task Decomposition: Turn "Write a 5,000-word essay" into 10-15 minute subtasks.
  • Nested To-Do List Structure: See your Main Goal and its detailed Step List in one glance.
  • Instant Motivation: Earn encouraging stars for every completed subtask.
  • Simple Status Tracking: Task titles clearly show incomplete, partially completed, or fully completed status.
  • Review & Reuse: Access and copy completed tasks from the History board to reuse productive plans.
  • Minimalist Design: Fast response and a clean user interface to prevent feeling overwhelmed by another demanding app.

Free Tier

Designed to help you focus deeply on one task at a time. Free users have the following limits:

  • One Active Task: You can only have one task running in your main view.
  • One Daily Input: Limited to one new AI task decomposition input per day.
  • Input Lock: The task input function is locked while an active task is present, strongly encouraging you to complete your current "small step" before moving on.
